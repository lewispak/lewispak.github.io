Every HTML element can pretty much considered a box. Paragraphs, images, and even healines can all be considered boxes. In CSS, this "box" is referred to as the term "box model" whenever we're talking about design and layout. It's a desgin model that is essentially a box that wraps around every HTML element. It's made up of:

  1. Margin
  2. Borders
  3. Padding
  4. Content

The Box Model Explained

  1. Margin - The area OUTSIDE of the boarder
  2. Borders - A bounary line surrounding the content and padding around it.
  3. Padding - The space between the content and and border.
  4. Content - The media being displayed on the web page.
Using the box model, we can define a border around any sort of content and then we can define the space between that border and content, or around that border.
